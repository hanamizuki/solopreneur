# Industry & Role-Specific Playbooks

> Last updated: 2026-04

This reference covers tailored LinkedIn growth strategies for six distinct
scenarios: B2B SaaS founders/marketing leads, consultants/coaches/freelancers,
job seekers, engineers/developers, recruiters/HR professionals, and
Taiwan/Asia market operators. Each scenario includes headline guidance,
content pillars, posting cadence, engagement tactics, KPI benchmarks, and
common mistakes. The final sections address Taiwan/Asia market specifics
and benchmark contradictions across published research.

Sources: Socialinsider 2026 (1.3M business posts), Edelman-LinkedIn 2025
B2B Thought Leadership Impact Report, Ipsos-LinkedIn 2025 B2B Marketing
Benchmark, Metricool 2025 (577K posts), academic mixed-method study of
1,001 personal posts (2026), DataReportal Taiwan Digital Report 2025,
LinkedIn engineering blog posts on feed ranking (2025-2026).

## 1. B2B SaaS Founder / Marketing Lead

### Headline Examples

**Good:**
- "B2B SaaS Founder | Helping manufacturing ops teams cut reporting time
  by 60% | Turning data into decisions with measurable processes"
- "Marketing Lead | ABM + product-led growth (PLG) | Turning content into
  a reusable customer acquisition asset"
- "Founder | Building category-leading [category] for [target role] |
  Proof-backed playbooks, not hype"

**Bad:**
- "CEO at Stealth" or "Helping businesses grow" — no audience, no outcome,
  no searchable keywords, no signal to decision-makers about what problem
  you solve.

### Content Pillars (3)

1. **Category/Trend POV ("Redefine the problem")**
   Edelman-LinkedIn research: 95% of hidden decision-makers say they are
   more willing to engage with sales/marketing from companies that produce
   high-quality thought leadership; 91% say a hallmark of quality TL is
   helping them see challenges or opportunities they had not recognized.
   Write about "the real risk your buyer hasn't noticed," not "our product
   is great."

2. **Reusable teaching assets (How-to / Framework / Checklist)**
   Hidden buyers prefer quick-to-consume highlights over deep academic
   long-form. 60% consider unique format or style a quality signal. Package
   your methodology as document carousels or downloadable templates — this
   outperforms manifesto-style essays.

3. **Evidence content with decision outcomes (Case study / Benchmark)**
   71% of hidden decision-makers say TL proves value better than traditional
   product materials; 64% trust TL over product catalogs. Frame case studies
   around before/after decision outcomes (risk reduced, cycle shortened,
   error rate dropped), not feature lists.

### Weekly Cadence

3 posts/week using a "two short, one long" rhythm:

| Slot | Format | Purpose |
|------|--------|---------|
| 1 | Document carousel | Template, framework, comparison chart, buyer evaluation checklist. Documents average ~7% engagement rate by impressions vs. 5.20% platform average. |
| 2 | Short video (30-90s) | Explain, don't advertise. 78% of B2B marketing leaders already use video; short-form video ranks highest in perceived ROI. |
| 3 | Text-only opinion post | Push your Category POV or dissect a common buyer misjudgment. Text posts have lower avg. engagement than documents but build topical continuity. |

### Engagement Strategy

- **Comment priority:** Target hidden buyers (finance, legal, procurement,
  compliance, ops) and direct buyers (tech/business leads). Hidden buyers
  rarely engage with sales — quality TL is the lever that makes them listen.
- **Comment style:** Add data, frameworks, or actionable next steps — not
  praise. LinkedIn distribution weighs meaningful discussion quality, not
  raw like counts.
- **Connection strategy:** Add 10-20 ICP contacts/week. Invite message:
  shared context + why you care about topics they care about. Never pitch
  product in the invite.

### KPIs & Benchmarks

**Influence layer (track weekly):**
- Engagement rate by impressions: 5.20% overall baseline; 7% for documents.
  Target: 4-week rolling average above baseline (consistency > single spikes).
- Format efficiency: documents should consistently outperform video and
  text-only (platform benchmarks confirm document and multi-image lead).

**Pipeline layer (track weekly/monthly):**
- Inbound DMs and booked calls from LinkedIn.
- Brand research signals: company page views, profile visits, mentions like
  "I saw your framework post."

> Note: Public data lacks unified "B2B SaaS founder leads" benchmarks.
> Use engagement rate baselines + your own historical MoM growth (e.g.,
> +20% MoM) rather than absolute lead count targets.

### Common Mistakes

- **Using LinkedIn as a bulletin board:** Constant product updates and
  company news. Academic research shows Business posts are common but
  Interpersonal posts generate more comments — B2B does not mean corporate
  voice only.
- **Hiding the product entirely:** The fix is framing your product as the
  concrete embodiment of your methodology, not hiding it. Edelman-LinkedIn
  defines TL as excluding content that "primarily describes products or
  services" — but you can still make people aware of what problem you solve.
- **Only running the company page:** Users and the platform prefer human
  voices. B2B Benchmark: marketers rank authenticity/credibility (58%) as
  the top criterion when choosing B2B creators; employees are recognized
  as an influencer type.

## 2. Consultant / Coach / Freelancer

### Headline Examples

**Good:**
- "B2B Growth Consultant | Turning marketing-to-close into a repeatable
  system | ABM, content funnels, sales enablement"
- "Career Coach | Helping mid-level managers land better offers in 90 days
  (portfolio + narrative strategy) | 1:1 & cohort"
- "Freelance UX Writer | Turning complex products into experiences that
  convert | Cases: onboarding conversion up, support tickets down"

**Bad:**
- "Consultant / Coach / Freelancer" — no audience, no promised value, no
  verifiable expertise boundary.

### Content Pillars (3)

1. **Diagnostic content (self-assessment tools)**
   Let readers determine whether they need you: scorecards, common-mistake
   checklists, decision trees. B2B research shows trust is the key to B2B
   success — make trust a perceivable system, not just a self-introduction.

2. **Consensus-challenging POV + actionable alternative**
   Hidden buyers prefer content that challenges assumptions and brings new
   thinking. For consultants, this is especially important — your product
   is literally "better judgment."

3. **Anonymized case studies focused on decision process**
   Deconstruct: starting state → intervention strategy → trade-offs →
   outcome. TL is trusted more than product catalogs for proving capability.
   You don't need client names — you need the reasoning chain.

### Weekly Cadence

3-4 posts/week, reserving time for engagement and DM conversion:

| Slot | Format | Purpose |
|------|--------|---------|
| 1 | Document carousel | Diagnostic tool, framework, SOP. Highest engagement format — ideal for "productizing methodology." |
| 2-3 | Short text posts | Problem → common misconception → your decision rule → one actionable step. Designed to trigger comments. |
| 4 (optional) | Short video | Talk about "how you judge," not "what you do." Short-form video leads in perceived ROI. |

### Engagement Strategy

- **Comment locations:** People your ideal clients already follow — industry
  analysts, domain creators, senior leaders in the same function. B2B
  research: authenticity/credibility is the top criterion for creator
  partnerships; your comments borrow from the same trust mechanism.
- **Connection sequence:** Engage 2-3 times on the same topic thread before
  sending a connect request. Show you are serious about the same problem.
  This aligns with LinkedIn's topical relevance logic.

### KPIs & Benchmarks

**Leading indicators:**
- Engagement rate by impressions: 5.20% baseline; 7% for documents.
  Highly vertical consultant content can sustain above-baseline rates
  because it attracts people with genuine pain points.
- Comment quality: look for "specific situation + follow-up question"
  comments — these signal genuine buying intent and align with the
  platform's meaningful conversation preferences.

**Conversion indicators:**
- Inbound DMs, form fills, meeting requests.
- Referral-driven conversations ("someone shared your framework with me").
  Research shows Observances and Business posts get more reposts, but
  consultants need precise-audience reposts into decision-making circles,
  not volume.

> Note: No public benchmark exists for "consultant inbound inquiry count."
> Track weekly qualified inquiries (ICP-match) + inquiry-to-close cycle
> length as internal baselines. B2B Benchmark acknowledges measurement
> and attribution gaps are normal.

### Common Mistakes

- **Content reads like advertising:** Emphasizing "I'm great" rather than
  "how I judge." Edelman-LinkedIn's TL definition explicitly excludes
  content primarily describing products/services; consultants sell judgment,
  so follow the definition even more strictly.
- **Locking everything behind a paywall:** In B2B contexts, TL is largely
  public or gated by registration. What you retain is not knowledge but
  contextualized, customized, hands-on execution.
- **No clear audience:** The more generic you are, the harder it is for
  LinkedIn's LLM-driven topic recommendation system to establish you as
  the go-to solution for a specific problem class.

## 3. Job Seeker / Passive Candidate

### Headline Examples

**Good:**
- "Target: Data Analyst / Analytics Engineer | SQL, dbt, Looker | Turning
  data into actionable decisions (portfolio pinned)"
- "Software Engineer (Backend) | Go / Python / AWS | Focused on
  observability & performance | OSS contributor"
- "Product Manager | B2B SaaS | Research & experimentation-driven growth |
  Shipped pricing & onboarding redesign"

**Bad:**
- "Open to work | Willing to take any opportunity" — unsearchable,
  uncategorizable, unmatchable for recruiters. Forces them to skip you.

### Content Pillars (3)

1. **Verifiable capability (project/portfolio breakdowns)**
   Deconstruct a project: problem, your role, decisions, results. Business
   posts are common on LinkedIn, but Interpersonal and Personal posts drive
   more comments — write projects as "discussable stories," not resume
   copy-paste.

2. **Learning journey with tangible output**
   Weekly "what I learned: one diagram + three pitfalls" posts. Satisfies
   the platform's preference for quick-to-consume formats and shows hiring
   managers how you think.

3. **Interpersonal content (gratitude, mentoring, community)**
   Not motivational fluff — 2026 research shows Interpersonal posts
   outperform on reactions and comments (i.e., higher visibility). Use
   this to stay visible without self-promotion fatigue.

### Weekly Cadence

2-3 posts/week + 10-15 min/day strategic commenting:

| Slot | Format | Purpose |
|------|--------|---------|
| 1 | Document carousel (portfolio/case) | Most effective for job seekers — makes your skills scannable, saveable. Highest engagement format. |
| 2 | Short text (learning + output) | Problem-oriented closing question to invite same-field comments. |
| 3 (optional) | Career reflection text | Keep Personal content anchored in "verifiable growth," not emotional venting. |

### Engagement Strategy

- **Comment locations:** Same-function people at target companies (team
  leads, senior ICs), plus recruiters' public posts. Domain creators'
  comment sections are "talent clusters" — Interpersonal posts generate
  the most comments, so position yourself in active discussions.
- **Connection strategy:** Add same-function/same-region contacts first,
  then target-company people. Enable "Open to Work" (can be recruiter-only
  visible) — this is an official mechanism that directly affects search
  appearance and inbound invitations.

### KPIs & Benchmarks

Job-seeker KPIs lack public benchmarks, so use directional indicators:

- **Search appearances:** LinkedIn provides data on who is searching for
  you — by job title and company. Target: your desired titles and companies
  start appearing in this list.
- **Inbound signals:** Steady weekly recruiter/hiring manager DMs or
  connection requests.
- **Content engagement:** Use 5.20% as overall engagement baseline,
  7% for documents. For search metrics, use "target companies appearing"
  as the success criterion rather than absolute numbers.

### Common Mistakes

- **Headline only shows current role with no target-function keywords:**
  You disappear from recruiter searches entirely.
- **Only posting "I'm looking for work":** May get sympathy likes but
  builds no topical credibility. LinkedIn's ranking increasingly
  emphasizes "what you talk about" and "topic consistency over time."
- **Self-promotion without engagement:** Interpersonal posts generate
  more comments; comments and discussion quality are platform-level
  ranking signals. Posting without engaging is a dead end.

## 4. Engineer / Developer / Technical Professional

### Headline Examples

**Good:**
- "Backend Engineer | Go / Kubernetes / Observability | Turning latency
  and incident rates into manageable systems"
- "Full-stack | TypeScript / React / Node | Focused on DX & performance |
  OSS contributions + technical writing"
- "ML Engineer | Recommendation systems & LLM applications | End-to-end
  from data to deployment"

**Bad:**
- "Programmer | Love learning new things" — no domain, no anchor topic
  for speaking invitations or collaboration.

### Content Pillars (3)

1. **Architecture & trade-offs**
   Write technical decisions as: context, constraints, options, trade-offs,
   outcome. This structure naturally invites discussion (comments), and
   LinkedIn distribution favors meaningful interaction.

2. **Visual teaching assets**
   Architecture diagrams + carousel breakdowns, incident timelines. Documents
   and multi-image posts outperform in engagement benchmarks because they
   are scannable, saveable, and revisitable.

3. **Build in public (accessible to non-specialists)**
   Hidden buyers prefer quick takeaways over deep academic content. If your
   goal is collaboration, speaking invitations, or consulting — your content
   must cross the "only understandable by peers" threshold.

### Weekly Cadence

Engineers most commonly fail with "high frequency, low density." Flip it:

| Slot | Format | Purpose |
|------|--------|---------|
| 1 | Document carousel | Architecture breakdown, debug checklist, performance optimization guide. Document engagement baseline: ~7%. |
| 2 | Short text post | Share an engineering trade-off or pitfall; close with a genuine question for peers to drive comments. |

If also active on X/Twitter: use X for real-time signal, LinkedIn for
cumulative content assets. X brand engagement rate benchmarks are
substantially lower than LinkedIn (direction consistent across studies,
though denominators differ).

### Engagement Strategy

- **Comment locations:** Technical community core figures (maintainers,
  staff engineers, DevRel, technical authors). Use "supplementary case /
  counterexample / benchmark data" to earn replies. Interpersonal posts
  outperform on reactions/comments — make technical content "replyable."
- **Connection strategy:** Comment first, then connect. Reference the
  specific point you discussed. This aligns with the platform's relevance
  and relationship signals.

### KPIs & Benchmarks

**Terminal KPIs (what matters most):**
- Speaking invitations, podcast/interview requests, OSS collaboration
  inquiries, consulting/freelance leads.

**Process KPIs:**
- Document engagement rate approaching or exceeding 7% (by impressions).
- Comment quality: "Can you expand on this?", "Is there a repo?",
  "Can you share the benchmark?" — depth of interaction matters more
  than volume. The platform explicitly avoids optimizing for virality
  and instead promotes relevant, helpful content.

> Note: No public benchmark for "speaking invitation frequency." Design
> as a four-stage milestone: engagement consistently above baseline →
> same-domain follower growth → collaboration inquiries appear → public
> invitations arrive.

### Common Mistakes

- **Code screenshots with no context:** LinkedIn ranking relies on
  understanding content topics; missing context reduces distribution
  to the right interest clusters.
- **Chasing depth at the expense of readability:** Research shows many
  audiences prefer quick takeaways. Preserve depth but lead with a
  scannable summary.
- **Not engaging:** The platform emphasizes meaningful comments and
  discussion quality. Post-only-never-reply makes it impossible to
  build a "community position" on any topic.

## 5. Recruiter / HR / Employer Brand

### Headline Examples

**Good:**
- "Talent Acquisition | Software & data roles | Skills + portfolio over
  credentials | Let's talk talent"
- "Employer Branding | Turning culture into verifiable content assets |
  Employee stories + career growth paths"
- "Recruiter | [Industry] | Helping candidates find better team fit,
  not just a faster offer"

**Bad:**
- "HR | Hiring" — reads like a bulletin board, no positioning, does not
  make top talent feel you understand what they want.

### Content Pillars (3)

1. **Role & skill clarity**
   Rewrite JDs from responsibility lists to "what success looks like" and
   "first 90-day deliverables." LinkedIn recruitment trend content
   emphasizes skills-based hiring and quality of hire; 93% of TA
   professionals agree accurate skills assessment is critical.

2. **Authentic employee stories**
   Interpersonal posts drive more reactions and comments — employer brands
   need candidates "willing to ask and chat." Employee-perspective posts
   ("why I stayed," "what I learned") outperform corporate-voice posts
   ("we're great").

3. **Verifiable trust signals**
   B2B Benchmark: 94% of marketers agree trust is key to B2B success;
   employer branding follows the same logic — candidates are doing risk
   assessment. Make trust evidence-based: career progression paths,
   training programs, compensation philosophy transparency, manager
   behavioral standards.

### Weekly Cadence

**Company page:** 2-3 posts/week (culture, roles, events). Prioritize
saveable formats (carousels, lists, FAQs).

**Personal profiles (HR, hiring managers, team leads):** 1-2 posts/week
+ daily engagement. Employees and managers are considered a trustworthy
voice; B2B research shows authenticity/credibility is the top criterion.

**Employee advocacy:** LinkedIn data (from official guides, still cited):
employee networks reach ~10x company page followers on average; even ~3%
of employees sharing content can contribute ~30% of total engagement;
employee-shared content typically sees ~2x the click-through rate of
company posts.

### Engagement Strategy

- **Comment locations:** Where your target talent hangs out. Engineers
  are in architecture/technical post threads; designers in portfolio
  teardowns; PMs in strategy/career posts. Interpersonal topics drive
  comments — intervene with genuine, specific remarks.
- **Connection strategy:** Tag by skills and portfolio, not credentials
  or tenure. Aligns with skills-based hiring trends.

### KPIs & Benchmarks

**Terminal KPI:** Quality of hire (LinkedIn recruitment content places
this at the center).

**Process KPIs:**
- Qualified applicant rate from LinkedIn.
- Employee advocacy participation rate (how many share/comment/collaborate).
- Company page engagement rate: use ~5.20% by impressions as a reasonable
  benchmark, but the real focus is "is the talent pool getting deeper?"

> Note: Qualified applicant count thresholds vary massively by company,
> market, and role difficulty. Provide tracking methods and denominator
> definitions rather than universal numbers.

### Common Mistakes

- **Company page only posts job listings:** Job postings already account
  for 13% of personal posts on the platform. Company-page-only job posts
  become noise.
- **Making employee advocacy a "repost KPI":** Employees will not sustain
  compliance-based sharing. The right approach: let employees share
  content that boosts their own credibility (what they built, learned,
  solved). This also aligns with the platform's preference for human
  voice and professional consistency.
- **Chasing follower count:** LinkedIn engagement research consistently
  shows engagement is a stronger influence signal than reach.

## 6. Taiwan / Asia Market Specifics

### Chinese vs. English Content Reach

LinkedIn's feed ranking considers post language, user behavior/preferences,
and career-goal relevance. If your network is mostly in Taiwan and you
publish long-form English content, initial high-quality engagement may be
limited — insufficient early interaction samples constrain subsequent
distribution (inferred from ranking mechanics, not a controlled study).

**There is no universal answer to "Chinese or English."** It depends
entirely on your target audience's location and language.

Operational recommendation from local practitioners: do not mix languages
within a single post (poor reading experience, audience skips). Instead,
split the same topic into separate language versions published as distinct
posts, allowing each to enter the corresponding audience's feed
independently. This is practitioner advice, not large-sample research.

### Taiwan Market Size

- DataReportal (Taiwan Digital Report, early 2025): LinkedIn ad reach
  equals approximately 19.1% of Taiwan's 18+ population.
- LinkedIn's Taiwan ad potential reach grew by ~500K (+13.5%) between
  late 2024 and late 2025.
- Third-party platform estimates place Taiwan LinkedIn users at
  approximately 4 million as of early 2026. These are estimates derived
  from ad data, not official member counts — label them accordingly.

**Implication:** LinkedIn is not a mainstream social platform in Taiwan,
but within the career and B2B context it is large enough to form a viable,
targetable professional audience pool.

### Trade Show Case Study (Business Cards → LinkedIn)

A Taiwan food brand case documented by Taiwan's official trade promotion
body shows:
- At overseas trade shows, the trend has shifted from exchanging business
  cards to adding each other on LinkedIn.
- After building a LinkedIn content presence, the company received
  unsolicited DMs from international buyers who discovered them through
  LinkedIn search — leading directly to online meeting bookings.
- For Taiwan B2B (especially export-oriented): your LinkedIn audience is
  not Taiwanese users but "international buyers who use LinkedIn to find
  suppliers."

### 104 vs. CakeResume Competitive Landscape

- **104:** Company filings cite SimilarWeb data showing significantly
  higher web traffic than other local job platforms. App downloads exceed
  10 million; cumulative talent pool and resume update volumes indicate
  dominant scale. If the goal is "high-volume applications" and "short-term
  job search," 104-type platforms remain the high-density venue.
- **CakeResume (now Cake):** Strong positioning in startup/international
  roles in Taiwan, with disclosed global user base in the millions. Cake
  is educating Taiwanese job seekers on portfolio-based personal branding
  habits.

**Strategic implication for LinkedIn in Taiwan:**
- Job seekers: position LinkedIn as "be found" and "be recruited
  internationally," not as an application-submission tool.
- B2B/export: position LinkedIn as "be discovered by international buyers"
  — an inbound channel, not an outbound tool.

### Data Gaps — What We Don't Know

The following questions lack consistent, large-sample public research as
of 2026. Treat them as hypotheses requiring your own A/B testing:

1. **Chinese vs. English post reach in Taiwan networks:** No controlled
   comparison study exists. Test by publishing the same topic in both
   languages as separate posts; compare 4-week rolling average engagement
   rate and audience geography breakdown.

2. **Asia-specific user behavior differences:** Most "Asia vs. West"
   LinkedIn behavioral comparisons come from marketing agency observations
   or cross-platform reports, not rigorous single-platform studies.

3. **Taiwan LinkedIn creator success benchmarks:** Top Voice is
   invitation-only and LinkedIn does not publish complete lists by country.
   Reliable Taiwan-specific cases are enterprise/export-oriented, not
   individual creator league tables.

4. **Optimal posting time for Taiwan audiences:** No large-sample
   Taiwan-specific study available. Standard recommendation: test
   weekday mornings (8-9am) and lunch hours (12-1pm) in your timezone,
   then optimize based on your own analytics data.

5. **DM response rates for Taiwan B2B outreach:** No published baseline.
   Track your own response rates and build internal benchmarks.

## Benchmark Contradictions

### Engagement Rate Denominators

Different reports produce very different numbers because they use different
denominators:

| Source | Year | Metric | Value | Denominator |
|--------|------|--------|-------|-------------|
| Socialinsider | 2026 | Avg. engagement rate | 5.20% | Impressions (1.3M business posts) |
| Socialinsider | 2026 | Document engagement rate | ~7% | Impressions |
| Hootsuite | 2025 | Industry benchmark range | 2%-4% | Per post (varies by industry); overall ~3.4% |

The contradiction is not about who is right — it is about the denominator.
When recording benchmarks, always specify the denominator (impressions,
followers, per-post). Do not compare Socialinsider's 5.20% against
Hootsuite's 3.4% as if they measure the same thing. And do not compare
either against X/Twitter's ~0.16% without noting the completely different
calculation basis.

### Document vs. Video ROI

- **Organic engagement:** Document and multi-image formats consistently
  lead in engagement rate benchmarks (Socialinsider 2026).
- **B2B marketing investment:** Video is considered essential; short-form
  video is perceived as delivering the highest ROI. B2B marketers track
  video-specific metrics (watch time, completion rate, CTR, lead gen).

These findings are not contradictory. "Highest engagement rate format"
is not the same as "best funnel-moving format." Practical guidance:
- Use **documents** for distribution, saves, and credibility building.
- Use **video** for trust-building and explaining complex products/processes
  (especially when showing how something works matters more than static
  frameworks).
- Do not abandon either format based on a single metric type.

### "Don't Sell" vs. "Let People Know Who You Are"

Edelman-LinkedIn 2025 defines thought leadership as content providing
expert insights, guidance, or unique perspectives — typically free or
gated by registration — that does NOT primarily describe products or
services.

This gives every role a consistent resolution: you can make people aware
of what you do, but you must lead with insight and methodology, not
features. The product is the concrete embodiment of your thinking, not
the other way around.
