# X/Twitter Profile Scoring Rubric

A standardized rubric for evaluating 8 key profile elements. Each element is scored on a 1–10 scale. The total score (max 80) determines overall profile rank.

**Why this matters:** Research from 2026 growth case studies shows that a fully optimized profile can increase follow-through rate (profile visit → follow) by 200–400%. Profile optimization is the highest-leverage single action for any account.

---

## 1. Profile Picture

| Score | Band | What to Look For |
|-------|------|------------------|
| 1–3 | Poor | Missing, placeholder, or blurry/pixelated image. Default egg avatar. |
| 4–5 | Below Average | Real photo but unprofessional — bad lighting, group shot, heavily filtered, or low resolution. |
| 6–7 | Decent | Clear headshot or logo, but generic — nothing that makes it stand out in a feed. |
| 8–9 | Strong | Professional-quality photo or custom illustration. On-brand colors/style. Instantly recognizable at thumbnail size. |
| 10 | Elite | 100k-account quality. Polished, distinctive, and perfectly aligned with personal brand. Would not look out of place on a top creator's profile. |

---

## 2. Banner Image

| Score | Band | What to Look For |
|-------|------|------------------|
| 1–3 | Poor | Missing entirely, or an irrelevant/random image with no connection to the account's purpose. |
| 4–5 | Below Average | Present but blurry, cluttered, or visually messy. Poor text placement or unreadable copy. |
| 6–7 | Decent | Clean and visually acceptable, but does not communicate a clear brand message or value proposition. |
| 8–9 | Strong | Delivers a clear brand message — shows what you do, who you help, or your core promise. Professionally designed. |
| 10 | Elite | Perfect synthesis with bio and overall vibe. Reinforces the brand story at a glance. Works as a billboard for the account's identity. |

---

## 3. Username / Handle

| Score | Band | What to Look For |
|-------|------|------------------|
| 1–3 | Poor | Contains random numbers, excessive underscores, or special characters (e.g., `@john_doe_8837`). Hard to type or remember. |
| 4–5 | Below Average | Functional but forgettable. May include unnecessary prefixes/suffixes or abbreviations that obscure identity. |
| 6–7 | Decent | Includes real name or a recognizable keyword. Clean format, easy to type and share verbally. |
| 8–9 | Strong | Memorable, branded, and concise. Matches handles across other platforms. Easy to tag and search. |
| 10 | Elite | Iconic single-word or short handle. Instantly associated with the person or brand. Premium namespace. |

---

## 4. Bio

| Score | Band | What to Look For |
|-------|------|------------------|
| 1–3 | Poor | Blank, meaningless filler ("Just living life"), or a string of random emojis with no substance. |
| 4–5 | Below Average | Attempts a description but unclear — visitor cannot quickly understand what this person does or why to follow. |
| 6–7 | Decent | Describes work or niche clearly, but lacks social proof, specificity, or a reason to follow now. |
| 8–9 | Strong | Hits all 4 essential elements: **What** you do + **How** you do it + **Who** you serve + **Proof** (metrics, credentials, features). Well-structured and scannable. |
| 10 | Elite | Concise, compelling, and impossible to scroll past. Includes a clear CTA (subscribe, click link, DM). Every word earns its place. |

---

## 5. Link

| Score | Band | What to Look For |
|-------|------|------------------|
| 1–3 | Poor | No link at all. A wasted slot. |
| 4–5 | Below Average | Link is present but dead (404), irrelevant, or points to an abandoned page. |
| 6–7 | Decent | Working link to a Linktree, personal site, or relevant page. Functional but no conversion intent. |
| 8–9 | Strong | Links to a professional website with email capture, newsletter signup, or clear next step for visitors. |
| 10 | Elite | Links to a polished site with an irresistible lead magnet, freebie, or resource. Optimized for conversion — every click has a purpose. |

---

## 6. Pinned Tweet

| Score | Band | What to Look For |
|-------|------|------------------|
| 1–3 | Poor | No pinned tweet, or pinned tweet is a generic/throwaway post with no strategic value. |
| 4–5 | Below Average | Something is pinned, but it is boring, outdated, or irrelevant to the account's current focus. |
| 6–7 | Decent | Valuable content (e.g., a thread or insight), but the hook is weak — does not stop the scroll or compel engagement. |
| 8–9 | Strong | High-quality content with a strong hook. Shows visible engagement data (likes, RTs, replies) that serves as social proof. |
| 10 | Elite | Viral-level pinned tweet — 100+ retweets, massive engagement. Serves as the ultimate first impression and credibility anchor. |

---

## 7. Follower Ratio

**Stage-aware note:** For accounts under ~500 followers, a poor follower ratio
is expected and normal — the growth playbooks recommend actively following
relevant accounts at this stage. Don't penalize early-stage accounts for this.
Apply the scoring below for accounts with 1,000+ followers.

| Score | Band | What to Look For |
|-------|------|------------------|
| 1–3 | Poor | Following count exceeds follower count. Signals mass-following or lack of audience traction. |
| 4–5 | Below Average | Roughly equal following and followers. No clear authority signal. |
| 6–7 | Decent | Followers moderately outnumber following. Moving in the right direction. |
| 8–9 | Strong | Healthy ratio — followers significantly outnumber following. Demonstrates genuine audience growth. |
| 10 | Elite | Strong follow-back discipline. Following fewer than ~200 accounts while maintaining a large follower base. Clean, intentional curation. |

---

## 8. Blue Checkmark (X Premium)

| Score | Band | What to Look For |
|-------|------|------------------|
| 1–5 | No Checkmark | Account does not have X Premium / Blue verification. Missing out on algorithmic boost, edit access, and longer posts. |
| 6–7 | Underutilized | Has the checkmark but does not leverage Premium features — no long-form posts, no formatting, no subscriber-only content. |
| 8–10 | Fully Leveraged | Actively uses Premium features: long-form articles, bold/italic formatting, extended posts, subscriber perks. Maximizes the algorithmic and credibility benefits of verification. |

---

## Profile Rank System

Sum the scores across all 8 elements (max 80) and map to a rank:

| Total Score | Rank | Description |
|-------------|------|-------------|
| 0–30 | **Beginner** | Profile needs significant work across most elements. Focus on the basics first. |
| 31–50 | **Growing** | Foundation is in place but major gaps remain. Prioritize weak spots to unlock growth. |
| 51–65 | **Pro** | Solid profile with room for optimization. Fine-tune details to stand out. |
| 66–80 | **Elite** | Top-tier profile. Maintain quality and iterate on what is already working. |
